☝ My name is Jialin Lu (陆家林 in Chinese). I also go with the nickname Lucy, as this name has been with me for years.


I am mainly interested in the interpretability of adaptive systems: being interpretable, not in a mathematical sense or statistical significance, but in a way that will enhance our mechanistic understanding. An interpretable model allows more control from human for better (rapid and stable) development.

My curiosity on interpretability starts with analysing and interpreting complex network systems, biological systems and artificial ones (machine learning systems). That said, one such thing that is most intriguing to me is machine learning-based interpretation of complex networks. I believed in mies van der rohe's less is more (partly because my undergraduate education in design), but I care more about more is different, which drives me to research.
However, I do hold a rather radical view on the matter of interpretability that, active human intervention is an essential ingredient for any actionable interpretation. Interpretability should always be a means towards constructive actions and discoveries, and never be the end goal itself. Because ultimately it is not about prediction; it is about discovery and utilization of patterns.


Currently I am doing Master's thesis in Simon Fraser University supervised by Prof. Martin Ester.

Thank you for going through this site all the way down here. At last I would like to introduce my personal preferences.

  • I don't like parametric average models, including DNN. At least in terms of the current training way, I don't like it.
  • I like rule-based models.
  • Sometimes I appreciate k-nearest neighbours
  • Even more rarely, I think Gaussian process is interesting. But I believe it will get popular again, as the covariance kernels should be more interpretable and much easier to manage and reuse, for rigorous examination and generalization.
